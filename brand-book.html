06 / Motion

Animation

Subtle, purposeful motion. Animations guide attention and provide feedback — never distract.

Hover Lift

Cards and buttons lift slightly on hover to signal interactivity.

translateY(-4px) · 0.2s ease

Scroll Reveal

Content fades in and slides up as it enters the viewport.

translateY(30px) → 0 · 0.7s ease

Pulse

Used for live indicators and loading states.

scale 1 → 0.85 · 2s infinite

Motion Principles

Duration

Micro interactions: 0.15-0.2s. Reveals: 0.5-0.7s. Stagger delays: 0.1s increments.

Easing

Use ease for most transitions. Avoid linear unless continuous animation.

Direction

Content enters from below (Y+30px). Never from the sides unless sliding panels.

07 / Voice & Tone

How We Sound

Lynq speaks like a sharp, friendly colleague — not a robot, not a salesperson. Clear, warm, and direct.

C

Clear & Simple

  • "Post jobs, track candidates, and understand what's working"
  • "One place for your jobs, candidates, and hiring data"
  • "No spreadsheets required"
F

Friendly & Human

  • "A hiring dashboard that's actually pleasant to use"
  • "We'd love to show you around"
  • "See what you'll be working with"
P

Problem-First

  • "Never lose track of where anyone stands"
  • "Know what's working and what's not"
  • "Stop juggling spreadsheets and inboxes"
A

Action-Oriented

  • "Get in touch"
  • "Ready to see your hiring clearly?"
  • "Contact Us" — not "Request a demo"

Writing Guidelines

Do

  • Use everyday language your users already know
  • Lead with the benefit, not the feature
  • Keep headlines under 8 words when possible
  • Use second person — "your", "you"
  • Be specific: "Create listings in minutes" not "Easy job posting"
  • Use active voice

Don't

  • Use jargon: "leverage", "synergy", "optimize"
  • Overpromise: "revolutionary", "game-changing"
  • Use passive voice: "Candidates are tracked" → "Track your candidates"
  • Write walls of text — break into short paragraphs
  • Use exclamation marks excessively
  • Sound corporate or stiff
